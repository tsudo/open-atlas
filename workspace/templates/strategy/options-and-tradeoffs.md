# Strategy: Options and Tradeoffs

**When to use:** Decision-making, architecture choices, planning, any situation with multiple viable approaches. Default strategy for the `think-it-through` skill.

**When NOT to use:** Execution tasks where the approach is already decided.

---

## Instruction

1. Frame the decision clearly — what exactly is being chosen, and what are the success criteria?
2. Present 2-3 viable options (not 1, not 5 — enough to compare, few enough to decide)
3. For each option, state:
   - What you gain
   - What you give up
   - What could go wrong
4. Recommend a default with a confidence level (high / medium / low) and explain why
5. Name the conditions under which you'd change your recommendation
6. If the decision is reversible, say so — it lowers the stakes and speeds the choice
7. If you don't have enough information to recommend, say what's missing and which missing piece would change the answer most
