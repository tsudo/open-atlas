# Open Atlas Workspace

This is your AI governance workspace. Everything your AI agent needs to navigate, create, and maintain structured work lives here.

---

## Start Here

1. **Read `BLUEPRINT.md`** — your AI's map of the workspace
2. **Fill in `context/human-operating-model.md`** — teach your AI how you think
3. **Try a template** — use `templates/prompt/analysis.prompt.md` on a real problem
4. **Create your first knowledge object** — document something you learned this week using `templates/doc/knowledge-object.md`

---

## What's Inside

| Folder | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `governance/` | Term definitions and conceptual framework ([taxonomy](governance/taxonomy.md)) — reference material, read when needed |
| `templates/prompt/` | Thinking patterns — analysis, decision support, knowledge extraction |
| `templates/doc/` | Document skeletons — decision records, knowledge objects, briefs, procedures, meeting notes, READMEs |
| `templates/schema/` | Metadata standards — [frontmatter](templates/schema/frontmatter.md), [knowledge object lifecycle](templates/schema/knowledge-object.md) |
| `personas/` | Behavioral profiles for your AI — [Open Atlas advisor](personas/per_open-atlas.md) included as a working example |
| `context/` | Human collaboration files — [your operating model](context/human-operating-model.md) |
| `knowledge/` | Durable reference material organized by topic (your growing knowledge base) |
| `projects/` | Your active projects (add your own) |
| `drafts/` | Work in progress — AI outputs, explorations, rough thinking |
| `archive/` | Completed or deprecated work — retained, not active |

---

## How It Works

**Structure is context.** The way you organize information determines what your AI can find, retrieve, and reason about. This workspace gives your AI:

- **A map** (`BLUEPRINT.md`) so it knows where everything lives
- **Templates** so output is consistent and builds on prior work
- **Metadata** (frontmatter) so documents carry machine-readable signals
- **A lifecycle** (draft → reviewed → canonical) so knowledge matures intentionally

---

## Your First Week

After setup, try these to build the habit:

- [ ] **Day 1:** Use `templates/prompt/analysis.prompt.md` on a real problem you're working on
- [ ] **Day 2:** Record a recent decision using `templates/doc/decision-record.md` — even a small one
- [ ] **Day 3:** Write your first knowledge object about something you learned this week
- [ ] **Day 4:** Review your `BLUEPRINT.md` — does it still match your workspace? Update it
- [ ] **Day 5:** Look at your `drafts/` folder — is anything ready to promote to `knowledge/`?

The goal isn't to use every template. It's to build the reflex: structured input → structured output → reusable knowledge.

---

## Customizing

This workspace is a starting point. Adapt it to your domain:

- **Add folders** for your work — `clients/`, `research/`, `writing/`
- **Create personas** for your domains — duplicate `per_open-atlas.md` and change the role, perspective, and rules
- **Extend templates** — add document types that fit your workflow
- **Update the blueprint** every time you change the structure
