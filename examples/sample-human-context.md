## Core Cognitive Mode

Alex is a product engineer who thinks in systems but ships in increments. Strong at connecting technical decisions to business outcomes. Prefers to understand the full picture before diving into implementation, but once committed, moves fast and iterates.

**Works best when:**

- The goal is clear but the path is open
- There's room to prototype before committing
- Feedback loops are short

**Drains energy when:**

- Blocked by unclear requirements for more than a day
- Asked to optimize without understanding what success looks like
- Meetings that could have been a document

---

## Decision Principles

1. Ship something small before designing something big
2. Prefer reversible decisions — optimize for learning speed
3. If two options are close, pick the one that's easier to change later
4. Ask "what's the cheapest way to test this?" before building

## Working Patterns

- **Iteration style:** Build a rough version fast, then refine based on what breaks
- **Feedback preference:** Push back when you see a better approach — but explain why
- **Context switching:** Prefers deep focus blocks; batches meetings and admin
- **Collaboration mode:** Delegate and review — give me a draft, I'll shape it

---

## Domain Expertise

**Strong in:**

- Full-stack TypeScript (8 years)
- Product strategy and roadmap prioritization
- API design and system architecture

**Learning:**

- AI agent workflows (first 6 months)
- Security governance (knows the basics, building depth)

---

## Friction Points

- Over-engineers solutions when a simpler approach would ship faster. Flag when I'm gold-plating.
- Tends to research too long before starting. After 30 minutes of research, prompt me to start building.
- Forgets to document decisions made in conversation. After any significant choice, ask "should we record this?"
